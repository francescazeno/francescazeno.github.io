Timeline

Years/Descriptions

  • 1838 - Sir Charles Wheatstone described Stereopsis and then constructed the Stereoscope; which used a pair of mirrors at 45 degree angles to the user's eyes, each reflecting a picture located off to the side. (REWORD THIS)
  • 1935 - Science fiction writer, Stanley weinbaum, created a fictional model for VR in his story Pygmalion's Spectables.
  • 1956 - Sensorama, the first VR machine, was created by Morton Heilig. It was later patented in 1962.
  • 1960 - Heilig patented the Telesphere Mask, the first head-mounted display.
  • 1961 - Headsight was created by Philco Corporation engineers, Comeau and Bryan. It was the first motion tracking HMD. Though, it was used for the military, not virtual reality.
  • 1965 - Ivan Sutherland presented his vision of the Ulitimate Display.
  • 1966 - Thomas Furness, a military engineer, created the first flight simliator for the Air Force.
  • 1968 - Ivan Sutherland and Bob Sproull created the first virtual reality HMD, The Sword of Damocles.
  • 1969 - Myron Krueger, a computer artist, developed a succession of "artificial reality" experiences using computers and video systems.
  • 1972 - General Electric Corporation built a computerised flight simliator with a 180-degree field of vision by using three screens around the cockpit.
  • 1975 - Krueger's VIDEOPLACE, the first interactive VR platform, was displayed at the Milwaukee Art Center.
  • 1977 - Aspen Movie Map was created by MIT. This program allowed users to virtually wanser through Aspens city, similar to Google Street View.
  • 1979 - McDonnell-Douglas Corporation integrated VR into its HMD, the VITAL helmet, for military use.
  • 1980 - StereoGraphics created stereo vision glasses.
  • 1982 - Sayre Gloves were created by Sandin and Defanti.
  • 1985 - Jaron Lanier and Thomas Zimmerman founded VPL Research, Inc. They are the first company to sell VR goggles and gloves.
  • 1986 - Furness developed a flight simulator between 1986-1989, known as the Super Cockpit.
  • 1987 - The Company Dimesion International created a software that could build 3D worlds in a PC.
  • 1989 - Scott Foster founded Crystal River Engineering Inc after receiving a contract from NASA to develop the audio element of the Virtual Environment Workstation Project (VIEW).
  • 1990 - Jonathan Waldern exhibited Virtuality, a VR aracade machine, at the Computer Graphics 90 exhibition in London.
  • 1991 - Computer Simliated Teleoperation - NASA
  • 1994 - SEGA VR-1 & VictorMaxx CyberMaxx
  • 1995 - Nintendo Virtual Boy & Virtual IO I-Glasses & VFX1 Headgear Forte
  • 1997 - Virtual
  • 2001 - SAS Cube which led to Virtools VR PAck
  • 2007 - Google Street View - Immersive Media captured that footage
  • 2010 - Google stereoscopic 3D Street View & Palmer Luckey, 18 yr old, created first prototype for Oclius Rift
  • 2012 - Oculus Kickstarter raised $2.4 million
  • 2014 - Facebook bought Oclius for $2 Billion & Sony announced Project Morpheus & Google - Cardboard & Samsung - Samsung Gear VR
  • 2015 - Wall Street Journal launched VR roller coaster & BBC 360 video migrant camp & Washington Post VR expereince of Oval Office & RYOT vr film & Gloveone had successfli kickstarter campaign
  • 2016 - Most headsets had dynamic binaural audio & Haptic interfaces underdeveloped & HTC released HTC VIVE Steam VR headset w/ first commercial release of headset w/ sensor-based tracking
  • 2017 - Several companies developing VR including HTC, Google, Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, Sony, Samsung, etc
  • 2018 - Facebook demonstrated new prototype, the Half Dome.
  • 2019 - Oclius Rift S, Oclius Quest no cables, Quest can connect to pc, vive cosmos, pro eye, focus plus, Valve
